# LuminoLearn Academy — Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** January 7, 2025
**Last Updated:** January 7, 2025
**Applies To:** luminolearn.org and all LuminoLearn Academy services

---

## 1. Introduction

LuminoLearn Academy ("LuminoLearn," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of all individuals who interact with our services — particularly the children and youth we serve. We recognize that the personal information of children and young people is especially sensitive and must be handled with the highest standard of care.

This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, how we use and protect it, and what rights you have regarding your information. It applies to our website (luminolearn.org), our learning platform, and all related services.

LuminoLearn Academy operates in Ontario, Canada, and complies with the Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and applicable provincial privacy legislation. We are guided by the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada's recommendations on children's privacy, including the principle that the best interests of the child must be paramount in all decisions involving their personal information.

## 2. Definitions

- **"Student"** means a child or young person (ages 9–17) enrolled in a LuminoLearn program.
- **"Parent"** means the parent, legal guardian, or authorized caregiver of a Student.
- **"User"** means any individual who visits our website or uses our services, including Parents, Students, and professional learners.
- **"Personal information"** means information about an identifiable individual, as defined by PIPEDA.
- **"Learning data"** means information generated through a Student's participation in LuminoLearn programs, including assessment results, progress records, assignments, and teacher observations.

---

## 3. Information We Collect

We collect only the personal information that is necessary to provide our educational services. We do not collect information for advertising, profiling, or any purpose unrelated to education.

### 3.1 Information Provided by Parents

When you book a discovery call, submit an enrollment request, or communicate with us, we may collect:

- Parent/guardian full name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Child's name
- Child's age or date of birth
- Preferred subject(s) and program
- Any additional information you voluntarily provide about your child's learning needs, goals, or special requirements

### 3.2 Information Collected During Enrollment and Instruction

Once a Student is enrolled, we may collect:

- Student name and age group
- Assessment results from LuminoStart™ and ongoing evaluations
- Learning progress data (assignments completed, scores, skill mastery levels)
- Teacher observation notes and feedback
- Student work samples (projects, written assignments, code)
- Attendance records
- Communication between Parents and LuminoLearn staff

### 3.3 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect limited technical information:

- IP address (anonymized where possible)
- Browser type and version
- Pages visited and time spent on site
- Referring website
- Device type (desktop, mobile, tablet)

We use this information solely to improve website functionality and user experience. We do not use this data to identify individual visitors or to build user profiles.

### 3.4 Information We Do NOT Collect

- We do not collect biometric data from Students
- We do not collect location data beyond country/province
- We do not require Students to create social profiles or share personal details with other Students
- We do not collect information from third-party social media platforms
- We do not use cookies for advertising or cross-site tracking

---

## 4. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information only for the following purposes, which are identified at or before the time of collection:

### 4.1 Educational Service Delivery

- Assessing a Student's current level and designing a personalized learning path
- Delivering instruction, assignments, and projects
- Tracking Student progress and generating progress reports for Parents
- Enabling teachers to prepare for and adapt sessions based on Student needs
- Issuing certificates of completion

### 4.2 Communication with Families

- Scheduling discovery calls and sessions
- Sending progress updates and teacher feedback to Parents
- Responding to inquiries and support requests
- Sending essential service notifications (schedule changes, program updates)

### 4.3 Program Improvement

- Analyzing aggregate, de-identified learning data to improve curriculum design and instructional methods
- Evaluating program effectiveness and Student outcomes at a group level

### 4.4 Legal and Safety Obligations

- Complying with applicable laws and regulations
- Protecting the safety and wellbeing of Students and staff
- Responding to lawful requests from authorities, if required

**We do not use personal information for:**
- Advertising or marketing to Students
- Selling or renting to any third party
- Building behavioral profiles of Students
- Training artificial intelligence models
- Any purpose unrelated to the educational services described above

## 5. Consent

### 5.1 Parental Consent

Because our Students are minors (ages 9–17), we obtain consent from a Parent before collecting, using, or disclosing a Student's personal information. Consent is obtained through the enrollment process, during which Parents are informed of the purposes for which information is collected and asked to provide their express agreement.

For Students under the age of 13, we rely exclusively on parental consent. For Students aged 13–17, we obtain parental consent and, where appropriate, also seek the Student's own understanding and agreement.

### 5.2 Withdrawing Consent

Parents may withdraw consent for the collection, use, or disclosure of their child's personal information at any time by contacting our Privacy Officer. We will explain the consequences of withdrawal (for example, we may be unable to continue providing services if essential information cannot be maintained). Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of any processing that occurred before the withdrawal.

### 5.3 Website Visitors

By using our website, you consent to the limited collection of technical information described in Section 3.3. You may disable cookies in your browser settings, though this may affect website functionality.

## 6. Disclosure and Sharing of Information

We do not sell, rent, trade, or otherwise disclose personal information to third parties for their own purposes. We share personal information only in the following limited circumstances:

### 6.1 Service Providers

We use a limited number of third-party service providers to support our operations. These providers process personal information only on our behalf and under our instructions:

| Service | Provider | Purpose | Data Processed |
|---------|----------|---------|----------------|
| Learning platform | Google Classroom | Lesson delivery, assignments | Student name, class materials, submissions |
| Forms | Google Forms | Discovery call and enrollment requests | Parent and Student contact information |
| Email | Google Workspace | Communication with families | Email content, contact details |
| Hosting | Render | Website hosting | Anonymized technical data |
| Payment | Interac e-Transfer (via banks) | Tuition payments | No payment data is stored by LuminoLearn |

We require all service providers to maintain appropriate security safeguards and to use personal information only for the purposes for which it was disclosed to them.

### 6.2 Legal Requirements

We may disclose personal information if required by law, regulation, or legal process (such as a court order or subpoena), or if we believe in good faith that disclosure is necessary to protect the safety of a Student, another individual, or the public.

### 6.3 With Parental Authorization

We may share Student progress reports, assessment results, or learning data with other educational institutions or professionals at the explicit request and written authorization of a Parent.

## 7. Data Storage, Retention, and Security

### 7.1 Where Data Is Stored

Personal information is stored on servers located in Canada or the United States (through Google Workspace and Render). Where data is stored outside Canada, we ensure that the receiving jurisdiction provides an adequate level of protection, consistent with PIPEDA requirements.

### 7.2 How Long We Keep Data

We retain personal information only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected:

- **Active Students:** Information is retained for the duration of enrollment plus 2 years after the Student's last active program.
- **Inactive inquiries:** Contact information from discovery call requests is retained for 12 months, then deleted.
- **Learning records:** Assessment results, progress reports, and certificates are retained for 5 years after the Student's last program, to support future re-enrollment or reference requests.
- **Financial records:** Payment records are retained for 7 years as required by Canadian tax law.

Parents may request earlier deletion of their child's information, subject to our legal retention obligations.

### 7.3 Security Measures

We protect personal information with safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the information:

- Access to Student data is restricted to authorized staff (teachers, program coordinators, and administrators) on a need-to-know basis
- All staff with access to Student data sign confidentiality agreements
- Passwords and authentication are required for all systems containing personal information
- Data is transmitted using encryption (TLS/SSL)
- We conduct regular reviews of our security practices
- We maintain procedures for responding to data breaches, including notification to the Privacy Commissioner of Canada and affected individuals where there is a real risk of significant harm, as required by PIPEDA

## 8. Your Rights

Under PIPEDA, you have the following rights regarding personal information:

### 8.1 Right of Access

Parents may request access to the personal information we hold about them or their child. We will respond within 30 days of receiving a request. We may ask you to verify your identity before disclosing information.

### 8.2 Right to Correction

If you believe that personal information we hold is inaccurate or incomplete, you may request a correction. We will update the information and, where appropriate, notify any third parties to whom the information was disclosed.

### 8.3 Right to Deletion

You may request that we delete your child's personal information, subject to our legal obligations to retain certain records. Upon receiving a deletion request, we will delete or de-identify the information within 30 days, unless retention is required by law.

### 8.4 Right to Withdraw Consent

As described in Section 5.2, you may withdraw consent at any time.

### 8.5 Right to Complain

If you are not satisfied with our response to a privacy concern, you may file a complaint with the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada:

- **Website:** www.priv.gc.ca
- **Phone:** 1-800-282-1376
- **Mail:** 30 Victoria Street, Gatineau, QC K1A 1H3

## 9. Children's Privacy — Special Protections

We take additional measures to protect the privacy of the children in our care:

- **Data minimization:** We collect only the information necessary to provide educational services. We do not collect information "just in case" or for future unspecified purposes.
- **No advertising or profiling:** We never use Student data for advertising, behavioral profiling, or targeted content. There are no ads on our platform or website.
- **No social features:** Our platform does not include social feeds, public profiles, friend lists, or messaging between Students. There are no features designed to increase engagement through social pressure.
- **No gamification traps:** We do not use streaks, leaderboards, badges, or other engagement-maximizing features that exploit psychological vulnerabilities.
- **Teacher-mediated access:** Students access the platform through teacher-managed classrooms. They do not create their own accounts or manage their own data.
- **Parental visibility:** Parents receive regular progress reports and can request access to all data associated with their child at any time.
- **Future use:** Student learning data will not follow them into adulthood in ways they did not consent to. We will not share historical Student data with universities, employers, or other institutions without explicit parental (or, where the Student has reached the age of majority, the individual's) consent.

## 10. Use of Artificial Intelligence

LuminoLearn may use AI-assisted tools in the following limited ways:

- **Curriculum support:** AI tools may assist teachers in preparing lesson materials or adapting content to different skill levels. Teachers review and approve all AI-assisted materials before they are used with Students.
- **Assessment analysis:** Aggregate, de-identified learning data may be analyzed to identify patterns in curriculum effectiveness. This analysis does not involve individual Student profiles.

**We do not:**
- Use Student data to train AI models
- Deploy AI systems that make automated decisions about Student placement, grading, or progress without human teacher oversight
- Use AI to monitor, surveil, or behaviorally profile Students
- Provide Students with unsupervised access to generative AI tools as part of instruction for ages under 12

When AI tools are used in Computer Science instruction for Students aged 12 and older, they are used under teacher supervision as a subject of study — Students learn how AI works, not depend on it.

## 11. Cookies and Website Analytics

Our website uses a minimal number of cookies necessary for basic functionality:

- **Essential cookies:** Required for the website to function (session management, security). These cannot be disabled.
- **Analytics cookies:** We may use privacy-respecting analytics (no advertising trackers) to understand how visitors use our website in aggregate. No personally identifiable information is collected through analytics.

**We do not use:**
- Third-party advertising cookies
- Social media tracking pixels
- Cross-site tracking technologies
- Fingerprinting or device identification

You can control cookie settings through your browser. Disabling cookies may affect certain website features.
